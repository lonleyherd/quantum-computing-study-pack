# Quantum Computing Basics Quiz

Try answering from memory before checking below.

## Questions

1. What is the main difference between a bit and a qubit?
2. What does superposition mean in simple terms?
3. Why is it misleading to say a quantum computer just tries every answer at once?
4. What does measurement do to a qubit?
5. What is entanglement?
6. What role does interference play in a quantum algorithm?
7. What does a Hadamard gate often do to a qubit?
8. Why are today's quantum computers limited?

---

## Answers

1. A bit is either `0` or `1`, while a qubit can be in a combination of both before measurement.
2. A qubit can have amplitudes for both `0` and `1` at the same time.
3. The real advantage comes from shaping amplitudes so some outcomes become more likely and others cancel out.
4. It produces a classical result and collapses the quantum state to an outcome.
5. It is a quantum link between qubits where they must be described together, not independently.
6. It boosts useful outcomes and suppresses unhelpful ones.
7. It often puts a qubit into superposition.
8. Current hardware is noisy, error-prone, and still small in scale.
